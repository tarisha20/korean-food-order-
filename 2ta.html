our speciality

Kimchi

Probably the most popular of them all. Kimchi is considered the oldest and most important of all Korean dishes. These fermented vegetables are usually spicy with a bit of sourness. The side dish is typically prepared with other ingredients, a Korean meal isn't complete without Kimchi. The food is a favorite among Korean cuisine enthusiasts not just because of its distinct flavor, but also due to its nutritional value and low calorie content.

Miyeok Guk

Miyeok-guk is rare among Korean soups in that it has no spicy ingredients. The main ingredient is miyeok, also known as sea mustard. It is typically prepared from dried product, and is in appearance brown tangled strands. To prepare, the seaweed is rehydrated, drained, chopped, sauteed with garlic and sesame oil, then simmered in beef or fish stock.

Bibimbap

The elements of bibimbap vary according to their region of origin (Tongyeong, Jeonju, Jinju). Bibimbap basically is a mixture of rice, beef, soy sauce, namul (sauteed and seasoned vegetables), mushrooms, chili pepper paste (gochujang), and a fried egg.

Kimbab

is a Korean dish made from cooked rice and ingredients such as vegetables, fish, and meats that are rolled in gim—dried sheets of seaweed—and served in bite-sized slices.

Japchae

Japchae is known for its savory and sweet taste with a soft yet chewy texture. Japchae is a traditional Korean noodle dish typically served during lunch or dinner time. Some ingredients mixed in the noodles include beef, thinly shredded vegetables, stir-fried sweet potatoes, sugar, and soy sauce.

Bulgogi

Bulgogi is one of the most revered Korean meat dishes known around the world. Bulgogi is a juicy, grilled marinated beef that is sweet with a flavorful taste. Bulgogi is sometimes wrapped in lettuce and coated with a thick, spicy paste called ssamjang.
